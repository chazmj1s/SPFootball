Glossary
Projection Metrics
MAE Mean Absolute Error — average points difference between projected and actual score. Lower is better. MAE of 15 means projections are off by ~15 points on average.
MAE (snapshot) MAE calculated using the projection that existed at each week's snapshot — reflects how good the model was at the time of the game.
MAE (total) MAE using the final end-of-season projection for all games. Generally lower since it uses more complete data.
Winner Accuracy Percentage of games where the model correctly predicted the winner. 65%+ is strong for college football. Drops significantly for postseason games.
Spread Bias Systematic lean in projections. Negative = model projects scores too low on average. Ideally close to 0. Our consistent negative bias suggests the model underestimates scoring.
Vegas MAE Same metric but using the Vegas closing line. Used to benchmark our model — we're currently running about 3 points behind Vegas on MAE.
Vegas Open MAE MAE using the opening line rather than the closing line. Useful to see how much the market moves between open and close.
Rating Metrics
Power Rating Composite score representing a team's overall strength relative to all FBS teams for a given week. Higher is better.
SOS Strength of Schedule — weighted average of opponents' power ratings. Higher means a tougher schedule.
Z-Score How many standard deviations a team's performance is from the mean. Used to normalize comparisons across eras.
Seed Long-term baseline rating built from historical performance going back multiple seasons. Slow to change — represents a program's sustained quality.
Trend Short-term momentum indicator based on recent weeks. Rises and falls faster than Seed — reflects hot or cold streaks.
Pedigree Weighted blend of Seed and Trend — the primary rating used in game projections. Balances long-term program quality with recent form.
Portal Metrics
Portal Winner / Loser Teams grouped by whether they net-gained (Winner) or net-lost (Loser) talent through the transfer portal in a given offseason.
MAE Gap Difference between Early-season MAE and Late-season MAE for a group. A larger gap means the model takes longer to calibrate to portal-affected teams.
Early / Mid / Late Period Season split by snapshot week: Early = weeks 0–3, Mid = weeks 4–8, Late = weeks 9+. MAE typically drops from Early to Late as the model learns each team's true strength.
PortalDelta Net change in roster quality from transfer portal activity. Positive = net gain, negative = net loss.
RosterStrength Composite measure of incoming transfer talent relative to what was lost. Used to adjust pre-season power ratings.
